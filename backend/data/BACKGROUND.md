# Project Background

Cantonese (廣東話) is a major Chinese language/ dialect spoken manly in Hong Kong, Macau, and the Guangdong Province of southern China. It is also widely used in overseas communities across Southeast Asia, North America, and Europe. With a rich history rooted in Middle Chinese, Cantonese preserves many ancient phonological features that have disappeared from Mandarin.

廣東話係喺香港、澳門等中國南部城市使用既主要語言，亦係世界各地華人社區中廣泛流通。廣東話源自中古漢語，保存咗好多古老嘅聲母同韻母特徵。

Cantonese is a tonal language with six to nine tones, meaning that the pitch contour of a syllable can change its meaning entirely. For example, the syllable "si" can mean "poem (詩)," "to try (試)," or "time (時)," depending on its tone. The writing system mainly uses traditional Chinese characters, but many spoken words have no standard written form, leading to the creation of special colloquial characters (for example: 冇 mouh = "not have," 咩 me = question particle).

廣東話係一種聲調語言，一般有六至九個聲調，同一個音節因為聲調唔同，可以代表完全唔同嘅意思。例如「si」可以係「詩」、可以係「試」、亦可以係「時」。雖然廣東話主要用繁體字書寫，但好多口語詞冇正式嘅書面形式，所以有啲特別造嘅字，例如「冇」（無）、「咩」（疑問語氣）。

What makes Cantonese particularly expressive is its use of Sentence-Final Particles (SFPs), such as "啦 (laa1)," "喎 (wo3)," and "呀 (aa3)," which carry subtle meanings about mood, intention, or politeness. These small sounds can change a sentence's tone from neutral to friendly, doubtful, surprised, or persuasive.

廣東話其中一個最有特色嘅係句尾助詞（Sentence-Final Particles, SFPs），例如「啦」、「喎」、「呀」等，呢啲細細粒嘅聲音可以表達講者嘅語氣、態度、情緒，令句子更自然、生動。

Translation between Cantonese and English is difficult because English lacks direct equivalents for tonal, pragmatic, and emotional markers like these SFPs. Moreover, Cantonese relies heavily on spoken context, tone of voice, and interpersonal familiarity to convey meaning, which are not always expressed in words. As a result, literal translation often loses the nuance and cultural flavor that make Cantonese so vivid and personal.

廣東話要準確翻譯成英文好困難，因為英文冇同類型嘅聲調同語氣結構。廣東話嘅意思往往依賴語境、語調、同講者之間嘅關係，呢啲喺英文度未必有直接對應嘅詞或語法。結果就算直譯都會失去好多細緻嘅情感同語氣。
